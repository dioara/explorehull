# Hull's Best Restaurants: A Foodie's Guide to the City

**Category:** Food & Drink  
**Author:** ExploreHull Editorial Team  
**Reading Time:** 7 minutes

Hull's food scene has undergone a remarkable transformation in recent years. Once known more for its fish and chips than fine dining, the city now boasts a diverse culinary landscape that ranges from innovative modern British cuisine to authentic international flavors. The City of Culture year accelerated this evolution, bringing new confidence and investment to Hull's restaurant sector. Today, food lovers will find plenty to excite their palates, from waterfront dining at the Marina to hidden gems in the Old Town.

## Waterfront Dining: The Marina's Best

The Hull Marina has become the city's premier dining destination, with restaurants taking advantage of the waterfront location to create atmospheric spaces perfect for everything from casual lunches to special occasions.

**Thieving Harry's** on Humber Street has established itself as a local favorite, particularly for breakfast and brunch. The cafe serves excellent coffee alongside hearty breakfast options, from full English breakfasts to lighter continental choices. The relaxed atmosphere and quality ingredients have made it a go-to spot for locals and visitors alike. The outdoor seating area is perfect on sunny mornings, offering views across the marina.

For evening dining, the Marina offers several upscale options. **1884 Dock Street Kitchen** combines a stunning waterfront location with a menu focused on locally sourced ingredients. The restaurant occupies a beautifully converted warehouse, with exposed brick and large windows overlooking the water. The menu changes seasonally but consistently delivers modern British cuisine with creative twists. Seafood features prominently—appropriate given Hull's maritime heritage—with dishes like pan-seared scallops and locally caught fish prepared with skill and flair.

**Ambiente Tapas** brings Spanish flavors to the Marina, offering an extensive selection of traditional and contemporary tapas. The sharing-plate format makes it ideal for groups, and the restaurant's lively atmosphere, particularly on weekend evenings, captures something of the Spanish dining experience. The wine list focuses on Spanish and Portuguese selections, with knowledgeable staff happy to recommend pairings.

## City Center Gems

Beyond the Marina, Hull's city center harbors several excellent restaurants that locals treasure and visitors discover with delight.

**Hearth** on Trinity Square represents the higher end of Hull's dining scene. This restaurant takes a farm-to-table approach seriously, working directly with local producers to source the finest Yorkshire ingredients. The menu is concise but perfectly executed, changing regularly to reflect seasonal availability. Expect dishes like slow-cooked lamb shoulder, locally reared beef, and creative vegetarian options that showcase vegetables as stars rather than afterthoughts. The wine list is carefully curated, and the service strikes the right balance between professional and welcoming.

For something more casual but equally delicious, **Hitchcock's Vegetarian Restaurant** on Bishop Lane proves that meat-free dining can be exciting and satisfying. The menu draws inspiration from global cuisines, offering everything from Thai curries to Mediterranean mezze platters. Even dedicated carnivores often leave impressed by the flavors and creativity on display. The cozy, slightly bohemian atmosphere adds to the appeal, making it feel like a well-kept secret even though it's been a Hull institution for years.

**Ambiente** (the original location on Princes Avenue, distinct from the Marina tapas bar) offers Italian dining in an elegant setting. The menu covers Italian classics executed with authenticity—proper risottos, handmade pasta, and wood-fired pizzas. The restaurant has maintained high standards for years, building a loyal local following while consistently impressing visitors.

## International Flavors

Hull's cultural diversity is reflected in its restaurant scene, with authentic international cuisines well represented across the city.

**Furley & Co** on Princes Avenue brings Middle Eastern and Mediterranean flavors to Hull with style. The menu features mezze platters, grilled meats, and vegetarian options bursting with flavor. The restaurant's interior is beautifully designed, with warm colors and comfortable seating creating an inviting atmosphere. It's become a popular spot for both casual dinners and celebrations.

For Indian cuisine, **Raithwaite** on Newland Avenue has earned a strong reputation for quality and consistency. The menu goes beyond standard curry house offerings, featuring regional specialties and chef's specials that showcase the breadth of Indian cooking. The spicing is carefully balanced, and the kitchen accommodates dietary requirements without compromising on flavor.

**Tapasya** offers another excellent Indian dining option, with a menu that emphasizes fresh ingredients and authentic preparation methods. The restaurant's modern interior provides a contemporary setting for traditional flavors, and the service is attentive without being intrusive.

## Casual Dining Done Right

Sometimes you want great food without the formality of fine dining. Hull delivers on this front with several excellent casual options.

**Dope Burger** has become something of a Hull phenomenon, serving gourmet burgers that have earned a cult following. The menu features creative combinations alongside classic burgers, all made with quality beef and fresh ingredients. The loaded fries are legendary among locals, and the milkshakes provide the perfect indulgent accompaniment. Despite the name, this is proper cooking—the burgers are juicy, the toppings are generous, and the buns hold together (a detail that matters more than you might think).

**Nibble** on Princes Avenue offers all-day dining in a relaxed, welcoming environment. The menu covers breakfast, brunch, lunch, and dinner, with options ranging from hearty full English breakfasts to lighter salads and sandwiches. The quality remains consistent regardless of what you order, and the atmosphere is perfect for everything from solo working lunches to family gatherings.

For pizza, **Dough & Co** delivers authentic Neapolitan-style pies from a wood-fired oven. The dough is made fresh daily, the toppings are high quality, and the cooking technique produces that perfect combination of crispy base and slightly charred crust. The restaurant keeps things simple, focusing on doing pizza right rather than offering an overwhelming menu.

## Fish and Chips: A Hull Essential

No food guide to Hull would be complete without addressing fish and chips. As a historic fishing port, Hull takes its fish and chips seriously, and several chippies maintain the high standards the city demands.

**Holtby's Fish & Chips** on Hessle Road is often cited as serving some of Hull's best. The fish is fresh, the batter is light and crispy, and the chips are proper—thick-cut and fluffy inside. This is traditional fish and chips done right, the kind that reminds you why this dish became a British institution.

**The Chip Inn** on Princes Avenue offers a slightly more contemporary take, with a sit-down restaurant alongside the traditional takeaway. The menu extends beyond standard chippy fare to include grilled fish options and seafood platters, but the classic fish and chips remains the star.

## The Hull Patty: Local Delicacy

While exploring Hull's food scene, you must try a Hull patty—a deep-fried disc of mashed potato and sage that's unique to the city. These golden-brown delights are a Hull institution, typically served in a bread roll (a "patty butty") or alongside fish and chips.

The best patties come from traditional fish and chip shops rather than restaurants. **Boyes** and **Holtby's** both serve excellent versions. The patty is comfort food at its finest—crispy on the outside, soft and flavorful inside, and utterly satisfying. It's not sophisticated cuisine, but it's authentic Hull, and trying one is essential to understanding the city's food culture.

## Sweet Endings

For dessert or afternoon treats, Hull offers several excellent options. **Crepe Affaire** in the city center serves both sweet and savory crepes, with the sweet versions ranging from simple lemon and sugar to elaborate chocolate and fruit combinations.

**Deli No. 1** on Princes Avenue combines a deli, cafe, and bakery, offering excellent cakes, pastries, and coffee. It's perfect for afternoon tea or a sweet pick-me-up while exploring the city.

## The Verdict

Hull's restaurant scene punches well above its weight, offering quality and variety that rivals much larger cities. The combination of waterfront locations, historic buildings, and passionate restaurateurs has created a dining landscape that continues to evolve and improve.

Prices remain reasonable compared to major tourist cities—you can eat very well in Hull without breaking the bank. Many restaurants offer early-bird menus and lunch specials that provide excellent value, and even the higher-end establishments are priced fairly for the quality delivered.

The city's food scene reflects Hull's character: unpretentious, welcoming, and quietly confident. Restaurants here don't need to shout about themselves; the food speaks for itself. For food lovers, this makes Hull a rewarding destination—a place where you can discover excellent dining without the hype and prices that often accompany it in more famous cities.

Whether you're after fine dining, casual eats, international flavors, or traditional British fare, Hull delivers. The city's restaurants are ready to surprise and satisfy, proving that great food isn't confined to capital cities and tourist hotspots. Come hungry, explore widely, and prepare to be impressed.

**Dining Tips:**
- Book ahead for popular restaurants, especially weekend evenings
- Many restaurants offer early-bird menus (typically 5-7pm) with excellent value
- The Marina and Princes Avenue are the main dining districts
- Don't miss trying a Hull patty—it's a unique local experience
