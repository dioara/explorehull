# Coffee Culture: Hull's Best Independent Cafes

**Category:** Food & Drink  
**Author:** ExploreHull Editorial Team  
**Reading Time:** 6 minutes

Hull's coffee culture has blossomed in recent years, transforming from a city where chain coffee shops dominated to one with a thriving independent cafe scene. Today, Hull boasts numerous independent cafes serving quality coffee, from specialty roasters to cozy neighborhood spots. These cafes have become community hubs, places where locals meet, work, and relax, contributing to Hull's evolving identity as a cultural city. For visitors, they offer a chance to experience Hull's contemporary side while enjoying excellent coffee.

## Two Gingers: Specialty Coffee Pioneer

**Two Gingers** has established itself as one of Hull's premier specialty coffee destinations. Located in the city center, this cafe takes its coffee seriously, working with quality roasters and training baristas to extract the best from each bean.

The cafe's interior is modern and minimalist, with clean lines and plenty of natural light creating a calm, focused atmosphere. It's popular with laptop workers and students, but the quality of the coffee attracts a diverse crowd throughout the day. The menu extends beyond coffee to include teas, fresh juices, and a selection of breakfast and lunch options.

What sets Two Gingers apart is the attention to detail. Baristas take time to dial in each coffee, adjusting grind size and extraction time to optimize flavor. They're happy to discuss different beans and brewing methods with interested customers, but equally content to simply serve excellent coffee to those who just want their morning caffeine fix.

The food menu complements the coffee, with fresh pastries, sandwiches, and salads made with quality ingredients. The avocado toast has become something of a signature dish, executed with care and generous portions. Prices are reasonable for the quality offered, making it accessible rather than exclusive.

## Brew: Community Coffee House

**Brew** on Princes Avenue embodies the community cafe concept, serving excellent coffee in a welcoming environment that encourages lingering. The cafe has a neighborhood feel, with regular customers greeted by name and newcomers made to feel immediately welcome.

The coffee is consistently good, sourced from reputable roasters and prepared with skill. But Brew's appeal goes beyond the coffee itself—it's the atmosphere, the friendly service, and the sense of being part of a community that keeps people coming back. The cafe hosts occasional events, from acoustic music sessions to art exhibitions, reinforcing its role as a community space.

The food offering is simple but well-executed: fresh sandwiches, homemade cakes, and breakfast options that cover the basics without overwhelming with choice. The cakes, in particular, have earned a strong reputation, with the lemon drizzle and chocolate brownies frequently selling out by mid-afternoon.

Brew's interior is comfortable and unpretentious, with mismatched furniture and local artwork on the walls creating a lived-in, welcoming feel. It's the kind of place where you can settle in with a book or laptop for hours without feeling pressured to leave, but equally suitable for a quick coffee stop.

## Be Coffee: Minimalist Excellence

**Be Coffee** brings Scandinavian-inspired minimalism to Hull's coffee scene. The cafe's aesthetic is clean and uncluttered, with white walls, light wood, and simple furniture creating a calm, almost meditative space. This isn't accidental—the cafe aims to provide a peaceful environment where the focus is on quality coffee and mindful consumption.

The coffee menu is carefully curated, featuring beans from respected specialty roasters. The baristas are knowledgeable and passionate, able to guide customers through different origins and flavor profiles. Pour-over coffee is available for those wanting to explore single-origin beans, while the espresso-based drinks are consistently excellent.

Food is kept simple, with a small selection of pastries and light lunch options. The emphasis is on quality over variety, with each item chosen to complement the coffee rather than compete with it. The cinnamon buns, in particular, have developed a following.

Be Coffee attracts a clientele that appreciates the cafe's ethos—people seeking quality and calm rather than hustle and bustle. It's popular with creative professionals and anyone wanting a respite from the city's busier areas. The cafe's location makes it slightly off the beaten path, which adds to its appeal as a peaceful retreat.

## Caffeinated: All-Day Destination

**Caffeinated** on Newland Avenue serves as an all-day cafe, transitioning seamlessly from breakfast spot to lunch destination to afternoon coffee stop. This versatility, combined with consistently good coffee and food, has made it a neighborhood favorite.

The coffee is sourced from quality roasters and prepared with care, but Caffeinated doesn't take itself too seriously—it's approachable and welcoming rather than intimidatingly specialist. The menu offers familiar favorites alongside more adventurous options, ensuring there's something for everyone.

Breakfast is a particular strength, with options ranging from simple toast and jam to full cooked breakfasts and creative brunch dishes. The pancakes have earned particular praise, served with various toppings and generous portions. Lunch brings sandwiches, salads, and hot dishes, all freshly prepared and reasonably priced.

The cafe's interior is bright and cheerful, with colorful accents and comfortable seating. It's family-friendly without being exclusively so, managing to appeal to diverse customers from young professionals to families to retirees. Weekend mornings can get busy, but the efficient service keeps things moving.

## Humber Street Gallery Cafe: Art and Coffee

Located within the Humber Street Gallery in the Fruit Market area, this cafe combines quality coffee with a cultural setting. The gallery itself is free to enter and showcases contemporary art, making the cafe an ideal stop during a cultural day out.

The coffee is excellent, with beans from reputable roasters and skilled preparation. The cafe's setting within the gallery gives it a unique atmosphere—you can enjoy your coffee while surrounded by art, and the large windows provide views across the Fruit Market area.

The food menu is simple but well-executed, with sandwiches, cakes, and light lunch options. The cafe attracts a mix of gallery visitors, local workers, and residents of the nearby Marina area. It's particularly pleasant on sunny days when the outdoor seating area allows you to enjoy coffee while watching the activity of Hull's creative quarter.

## Coffee 31: Waltham Street Favorite

**Coffee 31** on Waltham Street has built a strong reputation through consistent quality and friendly service. The cafe is small and unpretentious, focusing on doing the basics well rather than chasing trends.

The coffee is reliably good, with a menu that covers the standard espresso-based drinks plus filter coffee. The baristas are experienced and efficient, producing quality drinks even during busy periods. Regulars appreciate the consistency—you know what you're getting, and it's always good.

Food offerings include fresh sandwiches, paninis, and a selection of cakes and pastries. The portions are generous and prices are very reasonable, making it popular with students and local workers. The atmosphere is relaxed and welcoming, with a regular clientele that gives the cafe a community feel.

## The Verdict

Hull's independent cafe scene reflects the city's broader cultural evolution. These cafes are more than just places to get coffee—they're community spaces, work environments, and cultural hubs. They represent a shift in Hull's identity, from a city defined primarily by its industrial past to one embracing creativity, quality, and community.

For visitors, Hull's cafes offer a window into contemporary Hull life. You'll encounter locals going about their daily routines, overhear conversations about city developments and cultural events, and experience the welcoming atmosphere that characterizes Hull's best independent businesses.

The quality of coffee in Hull's independent cafes rivals what you'll find in much larger cities, but without the inflated prices or pretension that sometimes accompanies specialty coffee culture elsewhere. Hull's cafes are approachable and welcoming, serving excellent coffee without making you feel like you need a degree in coffee science to order.

Whether you're a serious coffee enthusiast seeking the perfect pour-over or simply someone who appreciates a good flat white in pleasant surroundings, Hull's independent cafes deliver. They're proof that quality and community can coexist, that you don't need to be in London or Manchester to find excellent coffee, and that Hull's transformation into a cultural city extends to every aspect of daily life, including that essential morning caffeine fix.
