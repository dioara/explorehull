# Where to Stay in Hull: Accommodation Guide for Every Budget

**Category:** Travel Tips  
**Author:** ExploreHull Editorial Team  
**Reading Time:** 5 minutes

Choosing where to stay in Hull significantly impacts your visit. The right location puts you within walking distance of attractions, restaurants, and the city's character. The right type of accommodation matches your budget and preferences. Here's a comprehensive guide to Hull accommodation, from budget options to boutique hotels, helping you find the perfect base for your Hull adventure.

## City Center: Convenient and Central

Staying in Hull's city center puts you within walking distance of major attractions, restaurants, and transport links. It's the most convenient location for first-time visitors wanting to maximize their time.

**Advantages:** Walking distance to Old Town, museums, shopping, and restaurants. Easy access to train and bus stations. Plenty of dining options nearby.

**Considerations:** Can be noisier, especially weekend evenings. Parking can be expensive or limited. Less residential character than outer neighborhoods.

**Accommodation Types:** Chain hotels dominate the city center, offering reliable quality and familiar standards. Options range from budget chains to mid-range business hotels. Independent hotels and guesthouses are less common in the center but do exist.

**Recommendations:** Look for hotels near Queen Victoria Square or Ferensway for central locations. Check if parking is included if you're driving—it can add significantly to costs.

## The Marina: Waterfront Atmosphere

The Marina area offers waterfront views, quality restaurants, and a more relaxed atmosphere than the busy city center. It's particularly appealing for those seeking a more upscale experience.

**Advantages:** Waterfront location with pleasant views. Excellent restaurants within walking distance. Quieter than city center while still being close to attractions. Easy walk to The Deep and Humber Street Gallery.

**Considerations:** Slightly further from city center attractions (though still walkable). Fewer budget options. Can feel somewhat isolated from the rest of Hull.

**Accommodation Types:** The Marina has several hotels and serviced apartments, generally mid-range to upscale. Some converted warehouses offer unique character. Airbnb options include modern apartments with Marina views.

**Recommendations:** Choose Marina accommodation if you prioritize atmosphere and dining options over being in the absolute center. Check for parking availability if driving.

## Old Town: Historic Character

Staying in or near the Old Town immerses you in Hull's history. Cobbled streets, historic buildings, and atmospheric pubs create a unique environment.

**Advantages:** Historic atmosphere and character. Close to museums and historic attractions. Quieter evenings than city center. Authentic Hull experience.

**Considerations:** Limited accommodation options. Can be quiet in evenings outside pub hours. Some buildings are older with potential quirks.

**Accommodation Types:** Options are limited but include some boutique accommodations and Airbnb rentals in converted historic buildings. The unique character often compensates for fewer choices.

**Recommendations:** Book early as options are limited. Embrace the historic character—creaking floors and quirky layouts are part of the experience.

## Princes Avenue/The Avenues: Bohemian and Residential

The Avenues area offers a residential neighborhood feel with excellent restaurants, cafes, and independent shops. It's ideal for those wanting to experience Hull like a local.

**Advantages:** Excellent restaurants and cafes on your doorstep. Beautiful Victorian architecture. Residential neighborhood atmosphere. Good bus connections to city center. Pearson Park nearby.

**Considerations:** 20-30 minute walk to city center (or short bus ride). Fewer traditional hotel options. Street parking can be challenging.

**Accommodation Types:** Airbnb dominates this area, with Victorian houses converted to apartments or rooms. Some guesthouses and B&Bs. Few traditional hotels.

**Recommendations:** Choose this area if you want to experience Hull's residential side and don't mind being slightly outside the center. Check transport options to city center attractions.

## Budget Options

Hull offers several budget accommodation options for cost-conscious travelers.

**Hostels:** Hull has limited hostel options compared to larger cities, but budget travelers can find affordable accommodation.

**Budget Hotels:** Chain budget hotels (Premier Inn, Travelodge) operate in Hull, offering basic but clean and functional rooms at reasonable prices. Locations include city center and outskirts.

**Airbnb:** Often provides good value, particularly for longer stays or groups. Options range from private rooms in shared houses to entire apartments.

**University Accommodation:** During summer holidays, the University of Hull sometimes offers student accommodation to visitors. Basic but very affordable.

**Tips:** Book in advance for better rates. Check location carefully—some budget options are on the outskirts, requiring transport to reach attractions. Read reviews to ensure quality meets your standards.

## Mid-Range Comfort

Most visitors opt for mid-range accommodation, balancing comfort, location, and price.

**Chain Hotels:** Reliable options include Holiday Inn, Mercure, and similar brands. They offer consistent quality, convenient locations, and familiar amenities. Prices typically range £60-100 per night.

**Independent Hotels:** Hull has some independent hotels offering character and personal service. These can provide more unique experiences than chains, though quality varies.

**Serviced Apartments:** Particularly good for longer stays or families, offering more space than hotel rooms with kitchen facilities. Several options exist in the city center and Marina.

**Tips:** Compare prices across booking sites—rates can vary significantly. Check what's included (parking, breakfast, wifi) as extras add up. Read recent reviews to ensure current quality.

## Boutique and Upscale

For those seeking something special, Hull offers some boutique and upscale options.

**Boutique Hotels:** While Hull isn't known for luxury hotels, some boutique options provide character and quality. These often occupy converted historic buildings or offer unique design.

**Marina Hotels:** Some Marina hotels offer upscale amenities and waterfront views, providing Hull's closest equivalent to resort-style accommodation.

**Unique Stays:** Airbnb offers some unique options, from converted warehouses to architect-designed apartments.

**Tips:** Book directly with hotels for best rates and flexibility. Check cancellation policies. Ask about upgrades—boutique hotels sometimes offer deals.

## Practical Considerations

**Parking:** If driving, check parking availability and costs. City center parking can be expensive. Some hotels include parking, others charge extra, and some have no parking at all.

**Breakfast:** Some accommodations include breakfast, others charge extra, and budget options may not offer it. Consider whether included breakfast saves money versus eating out.

**Wifi:** Most accommodations offer wifi, but check if it's free and reliable, particularly if you need to work.

**Accessibility:** If you have mobility needs, check accessibility in advance. Older buildings may have limited facilities.

**Noise:** City center and Marina locations can be noisy weekend evenings. Light sleepers should request quiet rooms or consider residential areas.

## The Verdict

Where you stay in Hull depends on your priorities. First-time visitors often prefer city center locations for convenience. Those seeking atmosphere might choose the Marina or Old Town. Budget travelers can find affordable options, though they may sacrifice location or amenities. Those wanting to experience residential Hull should consider The Avenues.

Hull's compact size means even "inconvenient" locations are rarely more than a 20-30 minute walk or short bus ride from major attractions. This gives you flexibility—you can prioritize character, price, or specific amenities without being too far from what you want to see.

Book in advance for better rates and more choice, particularly during major events like Freedom Festival or Hull Fair when accommodation fills quickly. Read recent reviews to ensure quality meets expectations. And remember that accommodation is just your base—you'll spend most of your time exploring Hull, so don't stress too much about finding the "perfect" place. As long as it's clean, safe, and reasonably located, you'll be fine.
